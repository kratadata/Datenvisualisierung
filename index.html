Course Description

Data enable machines to replicate human expertise by categorizing and classifying information. This process often simplifies complex information into repetitive patterns, giving the impression of an intelligent system. However, datasets conceal extensive human labor, raise ethical questions, and often contain Western biases. To address these challenges, there is growing interest in open-source datasets based on license-free or synthetic data. This module will analyze these developments and teach students how neural networks can be trained for local, specific tasks.
